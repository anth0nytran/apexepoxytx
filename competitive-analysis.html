Now — First 30 Days

Google Business Profile: Your #1 Growth Lever

This is the single most important thing you can do right now. Your Google Business Profile (GBP) is how you show up in the local map pack — those top 3 results with the map that appear when someone searches "epoxy flooring near me." This is where 80%+ of local leads come from.

Set Up Your GBP the Right Way

If you haven't already, claim and verify your Google Business Profile at business.google.com. Then optimize every single field:

  • 1 Business name: Use your exact business name — "Apex Epoxy & Surface Systems." Don't keyword-stuff it (Google penalizes this).
  • 2 Primary category: Set to "Epoxy Floor Coating Service" — this is the most specific category available and directly matches buyer intent.
  • 3 Secondary categories: Add "Floor Coating Contractor," "Concrete Contractor," and "Flooring Contractor." These capture additional search queries.
  • 4 Service area: Add every city you serve — Cypress, Houston, The Woodlands, Katy, Spring, Tomball, Sugar Land, Pearland, Friendswood, League City, Humble, Kingwood, Conroe, Richmond. Google shows your listing to searchers in these areas.
  • 5 Business description: Write a 750-character description that naturally includes your key services and cities. Lead with what makes you different: "Owner-operated epoxy flooring company based in Cypress, TX. Beau & Haden are on every job…"
  • 6 Services: Add each service individually — "Flake Epoxy Garage Floors," "Metallic Epoxy Floors," "Commercial & Warehouse Coatings," "Patio & Outdoor Coatings." Include a short description and price range for each.
  • 7 Attributes: Enable every relevant attribute — "Veteran-led" (if applicable), "Locally owned," "Free estimates," "Online appointments."
  • 8 Photos: Upload at least 10 real project photos immediately. Google prioritizes listings with more photos. Include: your logo, a team photo of Beau & Haden on a job site, 3+ before/after shots, close-ups of flake & metallic finishes, and 1-2 exterior shots of homes you've worked on.

The Review Strategy That Wins

Reviews are the #1 ranking factor for the local map pack — and the #1 trust signal for buyers. Your competitors have years of reviews. You need to close that gap fast.

Your target: 25 five-star reviews in 90 days. That's roughly 2 reviews per week. This alone will transform your GBP visibility.

  • ✓ Create a review link shortcut: In your GBP dashboard, go to "Ask for reviews" to get your direct review link. Save it in your phone and share it after every job.
  • ✓ Ask at the right moment: The best time is during the final walkthrough when the customer is seeing their new floor for the first time and they're excited. Say something like: "We're a new local business trying to build our reputation — if you're happy with the floor, a Google review would mean the world to us."
  • ✓ Follow up by text: 24 hours after the job, send a quick thank-you text with the direct review link. Keep it personal and short.
  • ✓ Respond to every single review: Thank each reviewer by name. Mention the specific service ("Thanks for trusting us with your garage floor!") and reference their city ("Great working in the Katy area"). This signals to Google that you're active and local.
  • ✓ Never offer incentives for reviews: It violates Google's terms and can get your profile suspended. Just ask genuinely and make it easy.

GBP Weekly Habits That Boost Ranking

Google rewards active profiles. Build these into your weekly routine:

Post Weekly Updates

Use GBP's "Updates" feature to post once a week. Cycle through these types:

  • • Project showcase — before/after photo with a short caption ("Just finished this 3-car garage in Cypress — full flake system with polyaspartic top coat")
  • • Offers — "Free estimates this week for Katy homeowners"
  • • Educational tips — "Why 100% solids epoxy lasts 5x longer than big box kits"
  • • Team photos — Show Beau & Haden on the job site

Upload New Photos Regularly

Add 2-3 new photos every week. Google tracks photo upload frequency as a ranking signal. Tips:

  • • Take photos of every single job (before, during, and after)
  • • Geo-tag your photos — make sure location services are on when you take them. This ties photos to the service area.
  • • Use descriptive file names before uploading: "flake-epoxy-garage-floor-cypress-tx.jpg"
  • • Variety matters — show different services, different homes, close-ups and wide shots

GBP Ranking Signals — What Actually Matters

Google ranks local map pack results based on three factors. Here's how to win each one:

Ranking Factor What It Means What You Should Do
Relevance How well your profile matches the search query Fill out every field. Use specific service names. Write a keyword-rich description. Add all categories.
Distance How close you are to the searcher You can't change your location — but adding all service area cities tells Google where you work. Mention city names in posts and review responses.
Prominence How well-known and trusted your business is online Get more reviews (volume + recency). Post weekly. Get listed on Yelp, BBB, Angi, and HomeAdvisor. Make sure your name, address, and phone (NAP) are consistent everywhere.

Pro tip — NAP consistency is critical: Your business name, address, and phone number must be exactly the same on your website, GBP, Yelp, Facebook, and every directory listing. Even small differences ("Suite 100" vs "Ste 100") can confuse Google and hurt your ranking. Pick one format and use it everywhere.

Months 1–3

Build Social Proof That Sells for You

Right now, your website tells visitors you're great. But there's no outside proof. Every top competitor in your market uses social proof as their #1 conversion tool. Here's how to build yours from scratch.

Document Every Single Job

Starting now, treat every project like a case study. This content becomes your most powerful sales tool over time.

  • ✓ Before photo — the ugly "before" is what makes the "after" stunning. Cracks, stains, oil spots — capture it all.
  • ✓ During photo — shows the process and craftsmanship. Grinding, coating, flake broadcast.
  • ✓ After photo — the money shot. Clean, glossy, beautiful. Multiple angles. Get one with the garage door open and natural light.
  • ✓ Short video clips — even 15-second phone clips of the finished floor with overhead lights on are powerful on social media.
  • ✓ Customer quote — ask the homeowner for a one-sentence reaction. Write it down. Get permission to use it.

Storage tip: Create a shared Google Photos album called "Apex Projects." Drop every photo there immediately. Tag by city and service type. In 3 months you'll have a library that most 5-year-old companies would envy.

Get Listed on Key Directories

Beyond Google, make sure Apex is listed on the platforms homeowners actually use when hiring contractors:

  • 1 Yelp — create a free business page. Many homeowners cross-check Yelp.
  • 2 Facebook Business Page — even if you don't post daily, having a page with your info and reviews matters for trust and GBP prominence.
  • 3 Nextdoor — hugely powerful for local contractors. Neighbors recommend businesses here constantly.
  • 4 BBB (Better Business Bureau) — signals legitimacy. Even a free listing helps.
  • 5 Angi / HomeAdvisor — optional, but they drive leads in the home services space.

Remember: Keep your NAP (Name, Address, Phone) identical on every single listing. This consistency is one of the strongest signals Google uses to rank your Business Profile.
